    Recovery & Sleep Optimization

    Sleep Protocol

    • Target: 8–9 hours per night
    • Consistent bedtime (10 PM)
    • No screens 1 hour before bed
    • Cool, dark room
    • No caffeine after 2 PM

    Recovery Techniques

    Active Recovery
    Light walking, mobility work, stretching on rest days
    Cold Exposure
    Cold showers (optional) — 2-3 minutes to reduce inflammation
    Breathing
    4-7-8 breathing: inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s — reduces stress

    Deload Weeks

    Every 8 weeks, reduce volume by 50% for one week to allow full recovery and supercompensation.

    Training Science

    Progressive Overload Principles

    • Increase reps before increasing difficulty
    • Master form before progressing
    • Add tempo variations (slow eccentrics)
    • Reduce rest periods
    • Add isometric holds

    Muscle Growth (Hypertrophy)

    Mechanical Tension
    Lifting challenging weights/bodyweight creates muscle damage that triggers growth
    Metabolic Stress
    The "burn" from high reps creates cellular swelling and growth factors
    Muscle Damage
    Micro-tears from training repair stronger (DOMS)

    Recovery Science

    Muscles grow during rest, not training. Sleep is when growth hormone peaks. Proper nutrition provides building blocks (amino acids).
